📈 Week 13: AI Metrics & Success Measurement

Measuring the business impact of AI

⏱️ Duration: 1 week
📚 Content: 4-5 hours
🎯 Level: Intermediate

🎯 What You'll Learn

ML Metrics

Accuracy, precision, recall, F1 score

Business Metrics

Revenue impact, user satisfaction, cost

User Metrics

Adoption, engagement, retention

Fairness Metrics

Bias and equity measures

The Metrics Pyramid

Three Layers of Metrics

Layer 1: ML Metrics (Lower Level)

Accuracy: % of predictions correct

Precision: Of predictions we made, how many were right?

Recall: Of all true cases, how many did we find?

F1-Score: Balance of precision & recall

Layer 2: Product Metrics (Middle Level)

Adoption: % of users using the AI feature

Engagement: How often users interact with AI

User Satisfaction: CSAT, NPS, thumbs-up rate

Task Success Rate: % of times AI helps users complete goal

Layer 3: Business Metrics (Top Level)

Revenue Impact: Did AI help upsell or reduce churn?

Cost Savings: Hours saved, support cost reduction

ROI: Cost of AI vs benefit generated

Customer Satisfaction: NPS, retention, churn rate

Fairness & Bias Metrics

Why measure fairness? Biased models cause real harm. You need to quantify it.

Key Fairness Metrics:

  • Demographic Parity: Same % approval rate across groups
  • Equalized Odds: Same true positive rate across groups
  • Calibration: Confidence scores same across groups
  • Disparate Impact Ratio: If <0.8, significant bias

Action: Measure fairness on test set. If disparate impact found, retrain with balanced data or adjust model.

Measuring Business Impact

The Hard Part: Causation vs correlation. If users are happier after AI launches, was it the AI or something else?

Best Approach: A/B Testing

  • Control group: No AI feature
  • Treatment group: With AI feature
  • Measure difference in key metrics
  • Statistically significant?

Fallback: Measurement Frameworks

  • Before/after comparison (less rigorous)
  • User surveys (explicit feedback)
  • Task success rate (proxy for impact)

✏️ Your Exercise This Week

Build Your Metrics Framework

  1. Define ML metrics (accuracy, precision, recall targets)
  2. Define product metrics (adoption, engagement, satisfaction)
  3. Define business metrics (revenue, cost, ROI)
  4. Choose fairness metrics and set thresholds
  5. Design your A/B test (if applicable)
  6. Create a metrics dashboard spec
