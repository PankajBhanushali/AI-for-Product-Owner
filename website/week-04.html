🗺️ Week 4: AI Opportunity Mapping

Identify where AI can create the most value in your product

⏱️ Duration: 1 week
📚 Content: 4-5 hours
🎯 Level: Intermediate

🎯 What You'll Learn

Opportunity Identification

Framework for spotting where AI can solve real problems

ROI Assessment

Evaluate business impact and implementation cost tradeoffs

Competitive Analysis

Understand what competitors are doing with AI

Prioritization

Choose the highest-impact opportunities to pursue first

The AI Opportunity Framework

Step 1: Map Your Product Journey

Break down your product into customer journey stages:

  • Awareness: How do customers learn about you?
  • Consideration: How do they evaluate you vs competitors?
  • Purchase: How do they buy?
  • Onboarding: How do they get started?
  • Usage: How do they use the product daily?
  • Support: How do they get help?
  • Retention: How do you keep them engaged?
  • Expansion/Referral: How do they grow with you?

Step 2: Identify Pain Points & Opportunities

For each stage, ask:

  • What's the customer's biggest frustration?
  • What takes the most time?
  • Where do we see the most churn/complaints?
  • What repetitive tasks could be automated?
  • Where could personalization add value?

Step 3: Assess AI Fit

For each opportunity, score it on:

  • Problem Clarity (1-5): Is the problem well-defined? (AI needs specificity)
  • Data Availability (1-5): Do we have enough quality data? (AI needs volume)
  • Business Impact (1-5): Does solving it matter to the bottom line?
  • Implementation Complexity (1-5): How hard to build? (1=easy, 5=hard)
  • Customer Delight (1-5): Will customers love it?

Scoring: Total score of 20+ = Strong opportunity. 15-20 = Maybe worth exploring. Below 15 = Skip for now.

📖 Real Example: SaaS Sales Tool Company

The Company: Builds sales CRM software. 50K customers. $10M ARR.

Opportunity Mapping:

  • Opportunity 1: Auto-transcribe and summarize sales calls (solves 30 mins/week of manual work)
  • Opportunity 2: Predict which accounts will close this quarter (helps reps prioritize)
  • Opportunity 3: Auto-generate personalized follow-up emails (saves time, improves response rates)

Scoring Results:

  • Opp 1: Problem (5) + Data (4) + Impact (4) + Complexity (3) + Delight (4) = 20 points → Pursue
  • Opp 2: Problem (4) + Data (2) + Impact (4) + Complexity (4) + Delight (3) = 17 points → Lower priority
  • Opp 3: Problem (5) + Data (5) + Impact (4) + Complexity (2) + Delight (5) = 21 points → Top priority

Decision: Start with Opportunity 3 (easiest, highest delight). Opportunity 1 as Phase 2. Re-evaluate Opp 2 after Phase 1.

💰 Quantifying Opportunity Size

For each top opportunity, estimate:

  • Time Saved: How many hours/week per customer?
  • Revenue Impact: Better retention? Upsell? Lower churn?
  • Implementation Cost: Engineering effort + infrastructure
  • Payback Period: How long until ROI breakeven?
  • TAM (Total Addressable Market): What % of customers could use this?

Rule of Thumb: If payback period is > 6 months, deprioritize unless strategic.

✏️ Your Exercise This Week

Create Your Opportunity Map

Using your product (or a product you know well):

  1. Map the customer journey (8+ stages)
  2. Identify 5-10 pain points
  3. Score each on the 5-point scale
  4. Rank by total score
  5. Write a 1-page summary of top 3 opportunities with business impact
