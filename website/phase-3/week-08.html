🎨 Week 8: AI UX Patterns

Designing intuitive interfaces for AI-powered features

⏱️ Duration: 1 week
📚 Content: 4-5 hours
🎯 Level: Intermediate

🎯 What You'll Learn

Common AI Patterns

Chat, copilot, autocomplete, recommendations...

Transparency

Showing users how AI decisions are made

Trust Signals

Building confidence in AI outputs

Error Handling

Graceful degradation when AI fails

Core AI UX Patterns

Pattern 1: Chat Interface

Best for: Complex queries, conversational help, exploring options

Design Considerations:

  • Show conversation history (so users remember context)
  • Display thinking time ("Analyzing..." spinner)
  • Make it clear which messages are from AI vs user
  • Offer suggested follow-ups or quick replies
  • Include feedback buttons (thumbs up/down)

Pattern 2: Copilot/Sidekick

Best for: Assisting with task at hand (writing, coding, designing)

Design Considerations:

  • Keep it in-context (next to the content it's helping with)
  • Easy toggle to show/hide without losing progress
  • Integration with main editor (apply suggestions easily)
  • Show what changed compared to original
  • Let users tweak suggestions before accepting

Pattern 3: Autocomplete/Suggestions

Best for: Speeding up repetitive tasks (search, form fill, code)

Design Considerations:

  • Show 3-5 top suggestions (not overwhelming)
  • Make it obvious it's AI (icon, color, label)
  • Let users dismiss/refine if not helpful
  • Trigger intelligently (not on every keystroke—annoying)
  • Keyboard shortcut to accept (Tab or Ctrl+Right)

Pattern 4: Recommendations

Best for: Help users discover what they need (content, products, next steps)

Design Considerations:

  • Explain WHY ("Because you liked X" or "Most popular for Y")
  • Show confidence level subtly (visual indicators)
  • Personalization should feel helpful, not creepy
  • Offer control (prefer less rec? customize preferences)
  • Track engagement and refine over time

Pattern 5: Alerts & Insights

Best for: Proactive alerts ("Your inventory is low", "Anomaly detected")

Design Considerations:

  • Be selective (only alert on high-confidence findings)
  • Surface in non-intrusive way (badge vs popup)
  • Provide context ("Why is this happening?")
  • Offer actions ("Dismiss", "Investigate", "Fix automatically")
  • Let users control notification frequency

🎨 Transparency & Trust: Design Principles

Show the AI's Confidence:

  • Visual badges: "Highly confident" vs "Not sure"
  • Probability scores when appropriate: "94% likelihood"
  • Uncertainty language: "This might be..." vs "This definitely is..."

Explain the "Why":

  • Hover tooltips explaining logic
  • "This recommendation based on: your browsing history, similar users, trending content"
  • Show supporting data when available

Enable User Control:

  • Ability to modify, refine, or override AI suggestions
  • Feedback mechanism ("This was helpful" / "Not useful")
  • Control center to adjust AI behavior

Be Honest About Limitations:

  • "AI gets this wrong sometimes. Always double-check."
  • Show what data the AI is seeing ("Based on: these 3 documents")
  • Clear disclaimers where needed ("Not medical advice")

⚠️ Handling AI Errors Gracefully

When AI is Uncertain:

  • ❌ Don't: Show no response or blank state
  • ✅ Do: "I'm not confident about this. Would you like me to escalate or try a different approach?"

When AI Gets It Wrong:

  • Provide easy correction ("Did you mean...?")
  • Learn from feedback ("Got it, I won't suggest that again")
  • Escalate when needed ("Connecting you to our human team")

Performance Issues:

  • Show progress ("Analyzing..." with realistic timeframe)
  • Allow cancellation ("Stop analyzing")
  • Fast fallback path ("Try search instead")

✏️ Your Exercise This Week

Design AI UX for Your Feature

  1. Choose the most appropriate AI pattern (chat, copilot, autocomplete, etc.)
  2. Create low-fidelity wireframes (5 key screens)
  3. Design the happy path + 2 error states
  4. Add transparency elements (confidence indicators, explanations)
  5. Create a design spec document with patterns and guidelines
