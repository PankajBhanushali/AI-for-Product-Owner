⚠️ Week 14: AI Risk Taxonomy

Identifying and categorizing AI risks

⏱️ Duration: 1 week
📚 Content: 5-6 hours
🎯 Level: Intermediate

🎯 What You'll Learn

Technical Risks

Model accuracy, data quality, system failures

Ethical Risks

Bias, fairness, transparency issues

Business Risks

User trust, brand damage, liability

Operational Risks

Security, compatibility, dependencies

The AI Risk Taxonomy

Five Categories of Risk

1. Technical & Performance Risks
  • Low accuracy: Model doesn't perform well in production
  • Data quality issues: Incomplete, stale, biased training data
  • Model drift: Performance degrades over time
  • Edge cases: Model fails on unusual inputs
  • Latency: Model too slow for product requirements
2. Ethical & Bias Risks
  • Demographic bias: Works worse for certain groups
  • Feedback loops: Predictions reinforce historical bias
  • Fairness violations: Unequal treatment between groups
  • Autonomy: Users lose control over important decisions
  • Transparency: Users don't understand why AI decided something
3. Privacy & Security Risks
  • Data exposure: Training data or predictions leaked
  • Membership inference: Attackers deduce if person in training set
  • Model extraction: Attacker reverse-engineers your model
  • API abuse: Model used for unintended purposes
  • Compliance: GDPR, data residency violations
4. Business & Reputational Risks
  • User distrust: People lose confidence in product
  • Brand damage: Negative press about AI behavior
  • Liability: Legal action if AI causes harm
  • Competitive: AI feature doesn't match competitor value
  • Adoption: Users reject feature due to concerns
5. Operational & System Risks
  • Dependency on 3rd party: API goes down, model becomes unavailable
  • Integration complexity: Hard to integrate into existing systems
  • Cost overruns: Model inference costs more than expected
  • Skill gaps: Team can't maintain/update the model
  • Version incompatibility: New model version breaks features

Risk Assessment Framework

For each risk, assess on two dimensions:

Likelihood (1-5): How likely is this to occur?

1 = Very unlikely, 5 = Very likely

Impact (1-5): How bad if it happens?

1 = Minor annoyance, 5 = Business-ending disaster

Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact

Then prioritize: highest scores get mitigation plans.

Mitigation Strategies

For high-risk items, choose mitigation:

  • Avoid: Don't use AI for this task at all
  • Reduce: Limit scope, add safeguards, increase monitoring
  • Transfer: Insurance, partnerships, outsource
  • Accept: Risk is manageable, monitor closely

✏️ Your Exercise This Week

Build Your Risk Register

  1. List all potential risks for your AI feature (at least 10)
  2. Place each in the appropriate category
  3. Rate likelihood (1-5) and impact (1-5)
  4. Calculate risk score
  5. For top 5 risks, define mitigation strategy
  6. Assign ownership and timelines
