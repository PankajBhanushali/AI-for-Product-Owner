⚖️ Week 15: AI Compliance & Governance

Navigating regulations and compliance requirements

⏱️ Duration: 1 week
📚 Content: 5-6 hours
🎯 Level: Intermediate to Advanced

🎯 What You'll Learn

Data Regulations

GDPR, CCPA, and privacy laws

AI-Specific Regs

EU AI Act, algorithmic accountability

Domain Compliance

Healthcare, finance, legal requirements

Documentation

Audit trails, model cards, impact assessments

Key Compliance Frameworks

1. Data Privacy (GDPR, CCPA)

Key Requirements:

  • Consent: Get explicit permission before using personal data
  • Transparency: Tell users data is being collected
  • Right to deletion: Users can request their data be deleted
  • Data security: Encrypt personal data, prevent breaches
  • Cross-border: Data location restrictions (EU data in EU, etc.)

For Product Owners: Ensure your AI doesn't train on personal data without permission. Have a data retention policy.

2. EU AI Act

Categorizes AI by risk level:

High-Risk (requires certification)

Criminal justice, employment decisions, credit assessment, biometrics

Medium-Risk (requires transparency)

Chatbots, recommendation systems, content moderation

Low-Risk (no restrictions)

Spam filters, autocomplete, general recommendations

Action: Determine your AI's risk level. If high-risk, prepare for third-party audit and certification.

3. Algorithmic Accountability

Document everything:

  • Model Card: What the model does, how it was trained, limitations
  • Data Sheet: Origin, composition, and characteristics of training data
  • Impact Assessment: Who is affected? What could go wrong?
  • Audit Trail: Who made what decisions about the model, when?

Why? If something goes wrong, you need to explain how the model works and why you built it that way.

4. Domain-Specific Compliance

Different industries have additional rules:

Healthcare (HIPAA)

Patient data must be encrypted and access-controlled. Model must explain recommendations.

Finance (SOX, MiFID)

Must document model rationale. Trading decisions need human approval.

Employment (EEOC)

No discrimination. Must audit for disparate impact.

✏️ Your Exercise This Week

Create Your Compliance Checklist

  1. Map your AI to EU AI Act risk category
  2. Identify applicable regulations (GDPR, industry-specific, etc.)
  3. Create a Model Card for your AI
  4. Document data sources and retention policy
  5. Draft an AI Impact Assessment
  6. Create audit trail of model development decisions
