Building Your AI Strategy

The AI Product Strategy Canvas

A framework to align your AI strategy:

1. Core Value Proposition

What specific problem does your AI solve?

Example: "Save sales reps 10 hours/week on email writing while improving response rates by 25%"

2. Target Customer

Who benefits most from this AI feature?

Example: "Mid-market B2B SaaS companies with 50+ person sales teams"

3. Key Metrics

How will you measure success?

Example: "Adoption rate (%), time saved (hours/week), NPS lift, retention impact"

4. Competitive Advantage

Why will customers choose us over competitors?

Example: "We have 5 years of customer email data. Our models are tuned for our product. No data sent to 3rd parties."

5. Risk & Mitigation

What could go wrong?

Example: "Model hallucination → Human review queue. Data privacy concerns → On-prem option"

Go-to-Market Strategies

Different strategies depending on your AI ambition:

Strategy 1: Silent Launch (Low Risk)

Enable feature quietly for 10% of customers. Gather feedback. If metrics are good, expand.

Best for: First AI features, uncertain market response, fast iteration

Strategy 2: Beta Program (Medium Risk)

Invite 100 high-engagement customers to test. Offer free access in exchange for feedback.

Best for: Building social proof, refining assumptions, creating advocates

Strategy 3: Public Launch (High Touch)

Announce feature in product, marketing, sales outreach. Train sales team. Create content.

Best for: Major features ready for scale, clear competitive advantage, market demand proven

💰 Pricing AI Features

How to price AI capabilities:

1. Value-Based Pricing

Price based on value generated (e.g., hours saved × hourly wage)

Example: If saves 10 hours/week worth $500/week = charge $100-150/month

2. Usage-Based Pricing

Charge per API call or per use (aligns with company's cost)

Example: $0.10 per email generated, $1 per meeting summary

3. Tier-Based Pricing

Include in tiers (Basic, Pro, Enterprise) with AI as differentiator

Example: Only Pro tier gets AI assistant. Only Enterprise gets custom models.

4. Freemium with Upgrade

Free limited access to drive adoption. Premium features require paid tier.

Example: 10 free suggestions/month, then $20/month for unlimited

⚠️ Common Strategy Mistakes

  • ❌ Launching AI features without understanding customer jobs-to-be-done
  • ❌ Over-promising model accuracy (setting unrealistic expectations)
  • ❌ Hiding AI behind the scenes (customers don't know they benefit)
  • ❌ Pricing AI features too low (undervalues capability)
  • ❌ Not planning for model drift (performance degrades over time)
  • ❌ Launching without human review for critical outputs
