The Executive Pitch Framework

The Narrative Arc (10 Minutes)

Tell a compelling story. Not a data dump.

Hook (1 min): The Problem

Start with a customer story or industry stat. Make them care. "Every day, 50% of our support tickets are repetitive questions costing us $500K/year."

Solution (2 min): How AI Solves It

Don't dive into how the model works. Keep it simple. "We built an AI chatbot that answers 80% of FAQs instantly, letting support focus on complex issues."

Results (3 min): Proof It Works

Pilot data, benchmarks vs competitors, customer feedback. "In beta with 100 users, chatbot handled 2,000 conversations with 92% satisfaction rating."

Business Case (2 min): ROI & Risks

Cost to build/maintain vs savings. "Build cost $200K, operational cost $5K/month. Saves $40K/month in support hours. Payback in 5 months."

Ask (2 min): What You Need

Be specific. "We need $150K Q1 budget and approval for 2 ML engineers to move to general availability by Q2."

The One-Slide Summary

For executives who will only look at one slide, make it count:

  • Top: Big headline/metric (e.g., "AI Chatbot: 40% reduction in support costs")
  • Left: The problem in 2 sentences + why it matters ($impact)
  • Center: Simple diagram or screenshot of the solution
  • Right: Key metrics in boxes: "92% satisfaction | 5 month payback | 2 FTE required"
  • Bottom: Ask: "Approve $150K Q1 budget?"

Stakeholder-Specific Messaging

Different people care about different things:

📊 CFO / Finance

Cares about: ROI, payback period, cost control. "This saves $480K annually with $200K investment."

🎯 CEO / Product

Cares about: Customer impact, competitive advantage, speed. "Competitors don't have this. First-mover advantage for 6 months."

⚖️ Legal / Compliance

Cares about: Risk mitigation, fairness audit, consent. "We've audited for bias and comply with GDPR."

👥 Support / Operations

Cares about: Implementation burden, support load, transparency. "Handoff plan: AI handles tier-1, escalates to you. You'll see 40% ticket reduction."

Handling Tough Questions

Common pushback and how to respond:

  • "Can't AI just hallucinate and give users wrong answers?"
    → Yes, that's why we guard guardrails: retrieval-augmented generation, fact-checking, escalation to humans for confidence below 90%.
  • "What about bias? Will it discriminate?"
    → Already audited. Fairness metrics show equal performance across demographics. Monitoring alerts if it drifts.
  • "Competitors will catch up quickly. Why invest now?"
    → First-mover advantage + customer data. 6 months head start, plus we'll have 1M conversations worth of training data they won't have.
  • "What if the model fails in production?"
    → Rollback plan: within 24 hours. Shadow mode first (users don't see it) for 2 weeks before general availability.
