Designing AI Assistants

The 5 Pillars of Good Assistant Design

1. Clarity of Purpose

What is this assistant for? Be specific.

❌ "AI Assistant" vs ✅ "Your customer support agent for billing questions"

2. Helpful Personality

Define personality: Professional? Friendly? Playful?

Create a persona: "Sarah, friendly support agent who always explains technical terms in simple language"

3. Smart Scope

Define what it can AND cannot do

"Can answer 200+ FAQ questions. For account issues, routes to human agent."

4. Graceful Failure

Design what happens when it doesn't know

Say "I'm not sure about that" rather than guessing. Escalate when needed.

5. Trust & Transparency

Be honest about what the AI is, limitations, when escalating

"I'm analyzing your data to find the best solution... connecting you to Marco for final review"

Designing Conversations That Work

Key Principles:

  • Listen First: Ask clarifying questions before assuming
  • Show Your Work: Explain reasoning ("It looks like you're trying to...")
  • Confirm Understanding: Ask "Is this what you meant?" before acting
  • Provide Options: When uncertain, offer multiple paths ("You could try A, B, or C")
  • Be Concise: Long AI responses feel unnatural
  • Match User Tone: Formal user → formal response. Casual → casual.

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

  • ❌ Over-apologizing ("I'm so sorry, I deeply regret...")
  • ❌ Using corporate jargon ("Leveraging synergies...")
  • ❌ Pretending to be human ("I was just analyzing...")
  • ❌ Long introductions ("Hello! I'm an AI assistant...")
  • ❌ Never admitting limitations (always makes something up)

📖 Conversation Flow Example

User: "My payment failed"

✅ Good AI: "I can help with that. Can you tell me which payment method failed (credit card, PayPal, etc)? That way I can give you the right solution."

❌ Bad AI: "I'm sorry to hear you experienced a payment issue. Please provide detailed information about your transaction."

User: "Credit card. I tried twice and both times it said 'declined'"

✅ Good AI: "Ah, a declined error usually means your bank is blocking it—either insufficient funds, fraud protection, or expired card. Here's what to try: [numbered list]. Or if that doesn't work, I can escalate to Marcus on our billing team."

🎭 Defining Your Assistant's Personality

Create an Assistant Persona Document:

Name: Alex (makes it feel human, approachable)

Role: Friendly product support specialist

Personality Traits: Patient, knowledgeable, straightforward, uses humor occasionally

Tone Examples:

  • "Totally! Here's how..."
  • "Quick question to help me figure this out..."
  • "That shouldn't happen—let me dig in"

Cannot do: Make promises, access production systems, discuss competitors

✏️ Your Exercise This Week

Design Your AI Assistant

  1. Define purpose: What should it do? Who is it for?
  2. Create a persona with name, traits, tone examples
  3. Design 5 conversation flows (happy path + error cases)
  4. Define scope: Write "Can do" and "Cannot do" lists
  5. Create a 1-page design document for your team
